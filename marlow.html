Product Design

/Marlow/

Creative direction and conceptualization

Overview

Athleisurewear brand, Marlow launched in 2020 with their first spring/summer collection. The Director of the company approached me with the task of shaping the creative direction for autumn/winter and designing and developing the collection.


I designed a 31 piece collection, along with a separate 5 piece knitwear capsule to be dropped in stores and online throughout autumn and winter 2021.

mood

Creative Direction

The overall feeling of the collection relates to finding a sense of calm and balance. Noting the small things arounds us. Finding beauty in every day objects such as the seam lines of a basket ball and the outlines and patterns found in tree bark. It's about slowing down and appreciating the world around us. Finding a balance between home-life, work and leisure.


Common themes found through the collection:


  • - Shape: trans-seasonal dressing from season to season.
  • - Silhouette: classic styles with a modern take.
  • - Functionality: active wear acting as day wear.
  • - Structure: fluidity and form to be gentle with subtle movement.
  • - Texture: smooth, gritty knits.
  • - Detail: elevated through subtle details such as line and contrast.

Conceptualization

Once the creative direction had been approved, I moved on to hand drawing concepts. Hundreds of drawings were created, as I iterated each piece in the collections. I created outfit boards to assess the collection as a whole. Looking at how each piece worked in with one another. Styling the collection in this way makes for a strong collection, ensuring each piece has a purpose and is different enough that the consumer would want to buy several pieces of the collection to outfit with. There is a mixture of fashion pieces and timeless basics. The basics are designed to be repeated in future collections, becoming Marlow staples.

Development

When the collection was designed and approved, I created computerized sketches and technical design sheets for each garment to be sent to the factory. When the factory had produced the prototype samples, I worked with the Director and Garment Technician to fit each sample on a model. Once we finalized the fit and design of each sample, we gave the factories the approval to start production.

Result

The collection was sold online and in store. The collection sold really well and a selection of the knitwear styles were made ‘best-seller’ and repeated the next collections.
